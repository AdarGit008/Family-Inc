# Family inc. — Kickoff Conversation Guide

*For Adar + partner. Set aside ~90 minutes, somewhere comfortable, no kids. The goal of this conversation is alignment, not decisions. You can change anything later.*

---

## Why we're doing this

Before the system tracks anything, you and your partner need to agree on what's worth tracking. A dashboard that surfaces things only one of you cares about will be ignored. A dashboard that surfaces shared priorities becomes a Sunday-evening ritual.

The questions below are ordered from easy to hard. Don't skip ahead.

---

## Part 1 — Quick wins (15 min)

These you both probably already agree on; just naming them out loud builds momentum.

1. **What's one thing in our lives we keep dropping the ball on?** (Examples: kids' dentist visits, car test, paying that one annual bill.) Write down the top 3.
2. **What's one thing we already do well that we want to keep visible?** (Examples: weekly date night, monthly savings transfer.) Write down 2-3.
3. **What's a recurring frustration we want this system to remove?** (Examples: not knowing what's on each other's calendar, surprise expenses, forgetting birthdays of extended family.) Top 5.

The answers to #1 and #3 become the first entries in the **Reminders** and **Calendars** tabs of the master sheet.

---

## Part 2 — Money (25 min)

The riskiest part of the system to get wrong. Be honest; the Sheet will see everything.

4. **What does "financially healthy" mean to us in 3 years?** A number, a feeling, both. (e.g., "180k₪ in savings + zero credit card debt + one paid-off car".)
5. **What are our current monthly fixed costs?** Rough number is fine. Mortgage/rent, daycare, insurance, utilities, subscriptions.
6. **What's our biggest known upcoming financial event in the next 12 months?** (Wedding, renovation, new car, school year start, vacation.)
7. **Which of us handles which money stuff today?** (Bill paying, budgeting, taxes, investments.) Write it down — even if it's "we both ignore X."
8. **Are there any money topics we avoid talking about?** Name them. The system will surface them eventually — better to know now.
9. **What's our spending "ouch threshold"?** The number that, if a single transaction crosses it, we want to be notified. (e.g., "any one charge above 1,500₪.")

Answers to #4-#6 seed the **Goals** and **Finance — Budget** tabs. #9 becomes an anomaly threshold in the monthly digest.

---

## Part 3 — The kids (20 min)

10. **For each child, name 1 thing we want to be on top of this year.** (Reading level, social skills, allergies management, switching preschool, etc.)
11. **Who's the first call for school stuff?** (You, partner, alternating, depends on day.)
12. **What's our current Kupat Holim setup per kid?** (Clalit/Maccabi/Meuhedet/Leumit, with which doctor.)
13. **What recurring health things are we behind on?** Dental cleanings, eye exams, well-child checkups, vaccines.
14. **What's the next big education milestone we need to plan for?** (1st-grade registration, daycare-to-preschool transition, etc.) Date if known.

Answers seed the **Education**, **Health**, and **People** tabs.

---

## Part 4 — Long-term goals (20 min)

15. **List up to 3 goals with a 3-5 year horizon.** Examples: "buy a house", "career change", "kids fluent in English", "60k₪ emergency fund", "renovate kitchen".
16. **For each goal, name the single most important next milestone in the next 90 days.** If you can't name one, the goal isn't actionable yet — make it more concrete.
17. **For each goal, who's the owner?** (One of you must own it; both-owns-it usually means no-one-owns-it.)
18. **What's one goal one of us has that the other hasn't really heard about?** (Trust me, ask. This is the question that surfaces the most.)

Answers seed the **Goals** tab and shape the Friday progress report.

---

## Part 5 — Operating agreements (10 min)

Quick agreements so the system doesn't cause arguments:

19. **Who gets WhatsApp alerts about what?** Default: both get everything. But maybe finance only Adar, kids only partner, etc. Decide explicitly.
20. **What's our "alert budget"?** Max # of WhatsApp messages per day from the system before it feels like spam. Recommendation: 2.
21. **Weekly review ritual.** When will we sit down together to look at the dashboard? Recommended: Sunday evening, with coffee, 20 minutes max.
22. **What's off-limits?** Anything we explicitly don't want the system tracking. (e.g., "no mood/wellness tracking", "no tracking of extended family conflicts".) Write it down.

---

## Output of this conversation

By the end, you should have a single page (write it down, then take a photo and drop it in the `Family inc.` folder) with:

- Top 3 dropped-ball items
- 3-year financial vision (one sentence)
- 3 long-term goals with owner + 90-day milestone
- Alert recipients per domain
- Daily alert budget number
- Weekly review time slot
- Off-limits list

That single page is what Hermes will use to populate the master Sheet. Without this, the Sheet is just empty tabs.
